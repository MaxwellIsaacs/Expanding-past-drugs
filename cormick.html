Bio

Gladys McCormick specializes in the political and economic history of Latin America and the Caribbean, focusing on corruption, drug trafficking, and political violence. She authored key works such as "The Last Door: Political Prisoners and the Use of Torture in Mexico's Dirty War" and "The Logic of Compromise: Authoritarianism, Betrayal, and Revolution in Rural Mexico, 1935-1965." McCormick is currently developing books on the history of torture in Mexico and drug trafficking in Latin America. She teaches various courses related to these subjects and has recently been appointed associate dean of diversity, equity, and inclusion at the Maxwell school. Her areas of expertise include modern Mexican history, gender, and rural experiences in Latin America. McCormick has held previous teaching positions and is currently an Associate Professor of History at Syracuse University and a Senior Associate at the Center for Strategic and International Studies.
